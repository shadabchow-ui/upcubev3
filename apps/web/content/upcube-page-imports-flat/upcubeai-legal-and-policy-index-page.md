# Policy status guide

## How to label pages responsibly.

Each legal or policy page should clearly identify its maturity.

### Final

Reviewed and approved legal copy is available. Entity details, dates, contact channels, and operational commitments are confirmed.

### Draft

Copy is prepared, but legal review or operational confirmation is still needed.

### Placeholder

The page exists for routing and structure only. It should not be treated as a binding policy.

### Product direction

The page describes the intended product philosophy or roadmap, not a legal commitment.

### Preview

The page describes a future or limited product surface. It should not imply general availability.

# Responsible publication rules

## Legal copy should never outrun legal review.

Before any legal or policy page is published as final, confirm that:

The source text is reviewed by qualified counsel.  
The policy matches actual product behavior.  
The page includes effective and updated dates.  
Contact channels are real and monitored.  
Product-specific claims are verified.  
Security and privacy claims are supported by operations.  
Compliance claims are backed by evidence.  
Third-party provider terms are understood.  
User rights workflows are operational.  
Retention and deletion statements are accurate.  
Billing and refund language matches the live product.  
Placeholder labels are removed only when final text is approved.
